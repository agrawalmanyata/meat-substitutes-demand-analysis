# Meat Substitutes Growth Analysis | Conagra Case Study

## Project Overview
This project analyzes growth opportunities in the U.S. meat substitutes category for Conagra Brands using machine learning, pricing elasticity modeling, and assortment optimization techniques.

The analysis was conducted as part of a predictive analytics consulting-style project focused on identifying strategic recommendations to improve product performance, distribution efficiency, and pricing strategy within the plant-based meat market.

---

## Business Problem
The meat substitutes category experienced rapid growth during 2020–2021 but has recently faced slowing demand, increased competition, and declining household penetration.

The primary objective of this project was to identify:
- Key drivers of product sales
- Pricing thresholds and elasticity behavior
- Distribution-related growth opportunities
- SKU assortment optimization strategies
- Competitive positioning gaps vs brands like Impossible Foods

---

## Analytical Techniques Used
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Feature Engineering
- Random Forest Modeling
- SHAP Feature Importance Analysis
- Log-Log OLS Regression
- Price Elasticity Modeling
- K-Means Clustering
- Distribution & Velocity Analysis

---

## Key Insights
### Distribution (ACV) Drives Sales
ACV Weighted Distribution accounted for nearly 95% of feature importance in predicting sales performance.

### Optimal Pricing Range
Highest product velocity occurred between:
- $3.50 – $4.50 price range

### Assortment Findings
Four SKU clusters were identified:
- Core Winners
- Hidden Gems
- Premium/Niche Products
- Underperforming SKUs

### Competitive Insights
Impossible Foods significantly outperformed Conagra in ACV expansion, highlighting distribution as the primary growth lever.
